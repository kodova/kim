Salsa Verde means "Green Sauce" in both Italian and Spanish.  However, they are two very different sauces.

What is Italian Salsa Verde?</h2>
In Italian cuisine Salsa Verde is a cold (or room temperature) oil and vinegar mixture loaded with fresh herbs such as parsley, basil, mint, or tarragon.  In addition it frequently is flavored with crushed garlic, anchovy or capers.  All the ingredients can either be chopped and mixed together or tossed into a food processor and blended together.  It can be served over cold, grilled, pan-seared or roasted meats, fish, salads, vegetables, or hard-boiled eggs.

What is Mexican Salsa Verde?</h2>
In Mexican Cuisine Salsa Verde is similar to the typical red salsa in its use as a dip for tortilla chips or served with tacos and grilled pork or beef.  The big differences is that it is more mild and it is green rather than red. The green base comes from green tomatoes known as tomatillos, jalapeños, and cilantro.
