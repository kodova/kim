Cooking Vocabulary</h1>
These are a few of the many cooking terms you may come across while reading

recipes.

 

Add</strong> - To put ingredients together; to put one ingredient with the others.

Beat</strong> - To mix quickly and continually, commonly used with eggs.

Blanch</strong> - Cook or dip quickly into very hot water to remove external material.

Blend</strong> - mix two or more items together until smooth.

Boil</strong> - cooking food until it reaches boiling point and lots of big bubbles form quickly.

Broil</strong> - cook directly under heat source in the oven.

Carve</strong> - To cut meat into slices.

Chop</strong> - To cut into small pieces, generally used with vegetables.

Coat</strong> – to cover completely with a thin layer.

Core</strong> – to remove the center and seeds from a fruit or vegetable.

Cream</strong> – beat two or more items together until smooth and creamy.

Cube</strong> – to cut into small squares.

Dot</strong> – placing bits of food such as butter on top of food being cooked.

Fold in</strong> - Mix together gently with the flat of the mixing tool.

Grate</strong> – cut food into very small pieces by rubbing it against a grater.

Grease</strong> – to apply a thin layer of butter or oil on the cooking surface to prevent sticking.

Knead</strong> – pressing, pulling and punching dough.

Mince</strong> - to grind food, normally meat, into small pieces. A machine is often used to do this.

Peel</strong> - To take the skin off of fruits or vegetables.

Preheat</strong> – heating the oven to the correct temperature before putting food in to bake.

Roast </strong>- To cook in the oven or over a fire.

Sauté</strong> - To quickly fry food by placing it in hot oil in a frying pan.

Scramble</strong> - To mix the white and yellow parts of eggs together while cooking them in a pan.

Sear</strong> - To drop food (usually meat) in a very hot pan to seal in juices.

Separate</strong> – usually referring to eggs, it is removing the yolk from the white.

Simmer</strong> – heat liquid till there are lots of tiny bubbles.

Slice</strong> - To cut into thin, wide portions.

Steam</strong> - To cook by placing the food above boiling water. Steam is the vapor that comes from hot water.

Steep</strong> - To let sit in hot water. (For example tea).

Stew</strong> - A very thick soup with a flour base.

Stir</strong> - To mix liquid ingredients by moving a spoon around in a circular motion.

Stir fry</strong> - To cook small pieces of food by moving it quickly in hot oil.

Toast</strong> - Lightly brown.
