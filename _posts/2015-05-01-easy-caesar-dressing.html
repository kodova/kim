Eating a side salad with dinner can either be boring or a lot more work than I want to do (especially when I'm already putting a lot of effort into the main part of the meal!).  If I don't have a bunch of veggies prepped to mix in with my lettuce, just a plain bowl of greens is not enjoyable.  So, if I'm going to eat just lettuce I want a salad dressing with lots of flavor.

A Caesar Salad is one dressing with enough flavor for a bowl of plain lettuce, but I had written off as unhealthy and one that I shouldn't eat... And I haven't for years!  I've considered making one from scratch in the past but did not want to buy anchovies or leave them completely out because I knew they add a needed flavor.  I recently came across the idea of using fish sauce as a replacement in a recent issue of Cooking Light Magazine.  Brilliant!  I already use it in other cooking, it is more approachable, affordable, and stays good for longer than anchovies.

One of the biggest ways people make a salad unhealthy is by using too much dressing.  Especially with thick and creamy dressing, like most store bought Caesar dressings.  A thinner dressing allows you to distribute the dressing better so you aren't consuming hundreds of calories of salad dressing.

Another common mistake with salad dressing is choosing a fat-free or light salad dressing.  The idea that fat causes heart disease and obesity has been disproved over the last few years.  In fact, fat can help your body absorb more nutrients.  So a salad dressing with fat in it will help you absorb the nutrients from the vegetables in the salad.

KIM Tips</h2>

  • Use two different oils - although it may be easier to just use one or the other, olive oil has a strong flavor and using too much may make the dressing unappealing and not using it at all may leave the salad dressing flat on flavor.</li>
  • Start buying your parmesan in wedges not pre-grated!  It will have more flavor and you can whatever texture you need: a fine grate on a microplane, thin shreds from the side of a box grater, or shave by using a vegetable peel.</li>
  • Store in a mason jar or other container with a tight fitting lid as the pepper settles to the bottom and you will want to give it a good shake before each use.</li>
  • If you miss the creaminess of your store-bought dressing, remove the water for a thicker texture.</li>
