Ramen noodles are very prevalent in Asian cuisine.  Ramen originated in China and became popular in Japan before Thai cuisine adopted it as a dish you can find on the street or markets late-night.  You may be able to find fresh Ramen noodles at an asian market, but I just used packaged Top Ramen without the seasoning.   These instant noodles are made of wheat flour that are cooked and then dried.  So once you get your boiling water it takes just two minutes to cook.

The rest of the dish is also quick.  While you are waiting for you water to boil, start prepping your seasonings, vegetables, and meat.  One tip to quickly slice your meat, whether pork, chicken or beef, is to put it in the freezer for about 15.  It will firm up the outside and allow you to quickly slice and get it extra thin so it cooks fast.

Not only is this dish quick it is also great as leftovers and cheap.  Packets of ramen noodles is frequently under a quarter each, you can use any cheap cut of meat, the veggies are reasonably priced, and you are using leftover cilantro stems.

You can use any Asian Green in this recipe: water spinach, choy sum, or bok choy.  I love baby bok choy as it is easy to handle and you can use just as much as you need and easily save the rest for another dish.  Bok Choy is a member of the cabbage family, so if you are not able to find it or any of the others greens in your market I would suggest using Napa cabbage which is also known as Chinese cabbage.
