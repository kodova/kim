Are you sick of giving your kids sugary cereal and frozen waffles for breakfast?

An essential in my pantry is my pancake mix</a> (rather than buying it pre-made you can make your own!).  But, mornings can be hectic and to remember to flip a pancake can be hard.  If you don't have kids, you might not understand how something so simple can be so difficult.

Enter: Pancake Muffins.  Use your typical pancake mix (try my homemade mix</a>!), stir in a little syrup and whatever you would normally top or mix in (blueberries as pictured above, chocolate chips, bacon), mix it together and portion into a mini muffin tray and bake.  Go make your coffee</a>, get the kids dressed, pack lunch boxes</a>, whatever else you need to do! Breakfast will be ready when you are.

My kids are incredibly unpredictable when it comes to how many pancakes they will eat. Sometimes they will eat two other times 5 or 6!  What is great about Pancake Muffins is that they are freezable, so you can make them ahead or freeze any leftovers.  Just cool completely and place in a freezer bag.  To reheat, microwave on high for 30 seconds at a time.

KIM Tips

  • Use a cookie or ice cream scoop to help evenly fill the muffin cups, I used the medium one in this</a> set for this recipe</li>
  • When you are Meal Planning (see ideas here</a> and here</a>), make the muffins on a batch cooking day</li>
  • You could do larger muffins, but cooking times will vary and I have not tried it.  You can find a mini muffin pan</a> on Amazon for about $12 or in most stores.</li>
