</a>What is next on the list for that leftover Slow Cooker Mexican Chicken</a>?  In case you missed it, I have made Tortilla soup</a> and tacos</a>.  For lunch today, I put a spin on Molletes - an open face bean and cheese sandwich. Before spreading the beans over the roll, I simply mixed in the leftover chicken.  Rather than going out to buy mexican queso, I used cheddar and jack cheese.  To serve, I topped it with the Pico de Gallo</a> I made for the tacos.  If you didn't make the pico, you can top with any salsa  (a great use for any leftover salsa from the original chicken recipe) or even pull out those hot sauce packets from your favorite fast food restaurant that you have been hoarding!

Molletes traditionally are served on rolls called bolillo.  It is similar to a french baguette, but shorter and a thinner crust.  Here in Arizona you can find this roll in almost all grocery stores.  Often it is baked fresh in the bakery where you can buy individual rolls.  If bolillo or another mexican bread like telera (which is more circular than oval and not as crusty) are not available, look for a similar bread that is savory and crusty on the outside.  Kaiser or ciabatta are great options.  Really, any sandwich roll would work.  Just flip it upside down and toast the outside in addition to the middle to help give the sandwich structure and texture.
