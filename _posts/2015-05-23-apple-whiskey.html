When I made my Apple Pie filling, I wanted a recipe that I could make once and then use in several different ways. This makes efficient use of my my time and ingredients while not eating the exact same thing over and over.  When I saw how many apple peels I had left, I didn't want to just toss them in the compost.  I spent time peeling the apples, and the skins are a perfectly good part of the fruit.

Flavored spirits are becoming more and more popular.  However, our liquor cabinet is full enough and I don't like to stock my pantry with ingredients I will rarely use especially when I can make smaller batches of them.  So I decided to make an Apple Whiskey, a flavored spirit that has been around for a long time.

I put the apple skins (can use any variety) in a glass jar with what was leftover of the cinnamon sticks from my apple pie filling, a clove, and covered it with whatever whiskey we had in our cabinet (bourbon would work too!).  Left it for a week, and strained it.  Use this recipe as a guide for proportions and make as much whiskey as you have apple skins - just make sure that the apples are barely covered.  Too many peels and the ones exposed to air may go bad.  Too much alcohol and you will not get as much apple flavor.

You can drink this straight, or use in any cocktail calling for apple whiskey, apple bourbon, apple jack, or even calvados.  Check back for ideas!
