I'm constantly trying to cook more from scratch.  Some recipes easily replace store-bought products, others fall short.  I have made several different granola bars but they have never been what I was looking for.  So I continued buying processed versions.  One thing I realized - my favorite store-bought varieties have some sort of puffed grain.  So although they were "chewy" they still have a crunch or crisp.  I also liked peanut butter flavored bars.  So I simply put those two items together and found this great recipe.

These granola bars are so easy to put together.  And the more you make them, the faster it will come together.  To speed it up, you can leave off the white chocolate topping, but it brings the bar to another level.

Granola bars are adaptable, so you can add additional ingredients like dried fruit, nuts, ground flax, protein powder, etc.  If you have never made a granola bar from scratch, my suggestion is that you follow the recipe the first time and then make adjustments the second time.

Whats awesome about these bars besides being delicious, easy to make, and easy to customize?  They also don't require an oven!!  Which may not be a big deal for some of you, but I live in Arizona and we are already regularly hitting 90+ (it is only April...)  I spend most of the year baking at night, early morning, or avoiding the oven all together; so it is nice to have a recipe that doesn't heat up my kitchen.

These bars can be healthy, but only as healthy as the individual ingredients you choose.  You can choose a brown rice cereal or one that is high in sugar; a natural peanut butter or one that has added sugar; a pure maple syrup or colored high fructose corn syrup.

KIM Tips:</h2>
You may not need to use all of the "liquid" mixture.  Add most of the liquids to the oats and puffed rice and stir to combine.  If it seems some of the oats aren't covered and still look dry, add the remaining liquid ingredients.

What is "puffed rice cereal"? You may know it as Rice Krispies!  There are many options now including brown rice and gluten free versions.  If you are trying to clean out your pantry</a> this is a great recipe to use the last of the cereal!

If your bars fall apart (as many of mine did) add more liquid ingredients next time or try storing in the fridge to keep the peanut butter and coconut oil in a more firm state.  Another option is to spray oil on top to add more moisture.

To make measuring the syrup and peanut butter easier swirl the coconut oil in the measuring cups first (melt the coconut oil if its solid).
