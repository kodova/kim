What makes me feel motivated at the start of the week is having my week of meals planned (check out my free printable!</a>).  I don't always follow it, but it prevents those moments of "I don't know what to cook" and I have more time throughout my week to accomplish other things (like share recipes and tips on my blog and twitter</a>).

Today, I'm going to share what I did last week to help my week go smoothly and not be in the kitchen for hours every day.

Sundays are my day to prepare items that have extensive prep, longer cooking times, or big batches to freeze and/or use throughout the week.  This week I made Pesto</a> (freeze extra), Hummus</a>, Marinara sauce (freeze extra), and double batch of meatballs (freeze half).

Early in the week I made a One Pan Chicken Parmesan & Zucchini</a>, which I served with the Marinara sauce I made on Sunday.  At the same time I baked a few more items - a time saver and because in Arizona summer is hot and I try to use the oven as little as possible!  I baked a spaghetti squash as an additional small side and for later in the week, a few extra Zucchini, and additional chicken (place chicken in a baking dish with chicken stock or water, cover and bake for about 15 minutes until cooked through).

I served the Spaghetti Squash with cooked italian sausage simmered in marinara sauce (I made regular pasta for the kids).

I used the extra zucchini with hummus in this wrap</a>.

The chicken was used as simple lunches for the kids, as a salad topper, and in this pesto chicken salad</a>.

I blended some roasted red peppers (frozen from a previous big batch cooking day</a>) with the pesto, spread it with cream cheese on a tortilla, placed on some sliced turkey breast and lettuce and rolled it to make pinwheels.
