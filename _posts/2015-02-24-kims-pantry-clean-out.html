I hardly have any room left in my pantry.  I kind of like grocery shopping, I can get obsessive from time to time with coupons, and occasionally impulse buy specialty items.  And when the kids are sick it seems like that is the only way I can get out of the house.  So, I buy a lot of groceries and occasionally need to step out of the grocery store and into my pantry for our meals.

A couple days ago I sat down and started to write out a pantry and freezer inventory.  I wrote down a list of my produce and some dairy and it was the length of my page.  I realized that although each item I bought was a good deal, I was just buying way too much.  That inspired me somewhat, and I looked at my canned foods and pulled out a can of artichokes that had been in there for a long time.  I managed to make two great recipes: Kale pesto pasta with Artichokes and chicken</a> and a Tuscan-Style Pizza</a>.  This feeling of success has further inspired me to clean out the pantry.

I am challenging myself to use up the stock in my pantry and freezer and minimize my grocery shopping for the next two weeks (or longer!).  The biggest challenge will be how to still create healthy, balanced meals so I do not gain a lot of weight!  I will still buy things, but keep it minimal.  I have decided I will buy salad greens, but no other salad toppings.  I can use beans, nuts, and other ingredients I already have.  Milk is another important item as I have two toddlers.

I want to share my experience and lessons learned with you.  I will post some of my recipes here, but be sure to follow me on Instagram</a> and Twitter</a> and like my Facebook</a> page for a closer look in my pantry and what I am eating! I hope you can learn from my mistakes and that you join me in this challenge (whether now or in the future).  Please share with me your pantry struggles!  Do you have any ingredients you don't know how to use up?
