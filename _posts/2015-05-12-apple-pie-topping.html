Apple Pie may seems like a flavor that is reserved for fall.  But unlike Pumpkin Pie, Apples season is long and is ending soon (although you can get apples all year).  Because it is the end of the season, some apples may be best used for cooking rather than eaten raw.  And who doesn't wish that after making apple pie that there is leftover filling?  This is a quick and simple stovetop recipe that can be frozen or used in a variety of ways - like in oatmeal or yogurt.  Check back to see other ways to use them!

KIM Tips</h2>
Choosing your apples:  The type is preference... choose what is cheap or what you enjoy.  I like granny smith for almost everything!  If you want to cut out the added honey, choose a variety that is sweet - cook it without the honey and taste (adding honey if its not sweet enough).  Choose apples that are firm and unbruised.  Shape doesn't matter!

Peeling: I use a paring knife to peel my apples.  It is small so it fits in your hand nicely.  First, I peel a section around the the stem. Then I peel 1/2 - 1 inch at a time down the side.  Then clean up the bottom.  Find out what I did with the leftover peels in this post</a> (it involves alcohol!).

Cutting: Cut off four sides around the core.  Lay flat and cut into 1/4-1/2 inch strips.  If your apple was fat, you might want to cut some strips in half.  Then cut cubes from each strip.

Ideas for using leftover Apple Pie</h3>
