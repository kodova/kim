The recipe title may sound intimidating - Salsa Verde and Scarlet Onions, how much time is this going to take! Truth is, only about 15 minutes.  It is a great introduction to Salsa Verde</a> (learn more about salsa verde here</a>) as it doesn't require a food processor and the ingredient list is short: only two chopped herbs, some oil and vinegar, and a portion of the Scarlet Onions, which are just quick pickled red onions that you already are making to top the asparagus with.

TOOL TIP:</strong>  This recipe calls for a large strip of orange zest that you need to slice into small strips.  You can do this with a paring knife or vegetable peeler.  You may have a standard citrus zester or grater, but I would recommend also having a "bartenders" zester (like this one</a>).  I like to use this zester for speed and appearance.  In fact this is the only zester I use for citrus.  Depending on your pressure you can get different types of zest.  If you run it quick and lightly over the peel you will get small pieces of zest that is commonly used.  For this recipe, use some pressure and you get long strands of zest like you can see in the picture above.  The zester does a great job at leaving behind the rind and it gives you perfectly even strips that are smaller than you could get without a lot of practice or meticulous work.   The OXO zester also has a notch took/channel knife that can be used for making fancy garnishes (we will get to that in another post!)
