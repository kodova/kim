Carrots are a great vegetable that is available all year at a reasonable cost. You can eat them raw, steamed, sautéed, or roasted. During the fall and winter I crave the deep savoriness from roasted vegetables (and the only time of year in the desert that having the oven on doesn't make the house feel like it is 90+ inside). This technique for roasting can be used for other root vegetables, too.
Carrots and cumin are a great pairing. But carrots are delicious with any of the spices listed below. Start with one seasoning the first time you make roasted carrots and then mix it up (I would use a combination of three or less, otherwise the flavors will muddle and you wont be able to get the great taste of the individual spices). Here I use cumin, which is one of my favorite spices.
