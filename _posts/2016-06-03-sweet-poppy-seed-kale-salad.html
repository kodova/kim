This is a "copycat" Sweet Kale Salad that is a kit I often buy from Costco.  I rarely crave kale salads, but the sweetness of the dressing and the dried cranberries really balances the bitterness of the kale.

Like a lot of kit salads and salads from restaurants, you have to be mindful of the ingredients and how much dressing is used.  If the salad is overdressed, you may consume as many calories as a chicken sandwich.

Making this salad from scratch allows you to control the dressing ingredients which helps makes it healthier.  You also control how much dressing is used.  If you don't like kale, you may be tempted to use a lot of dressing.  Before you do that, try using the tips below.  Then slowly add dressing, finding your preference.

KIM Tips

  • Cut your kale into small pieces, this will make it easier to chew</li>
  • Place your kale in a large bowl or container, sprinkle with salt and a squeeze of lemon juice.  "Massage" it - by that I mean grab a handful squeeze a little, release back in the bowl and repeat.  You will see the kale become a brighter green and wilt a little.  You can store kale like this for about a week! This method will break down the kale making it easier to chew and it will taste a little better.</li>
  • If the kale is still tough, let your salad sit for about 5 minutes after dressing it.</li>
  • The above tips are for any kale salad. For this specific salad, if you are in a pinch, you can buy a broccoli "slaw" as a substitute for the the matchstick broccoli.</li>
  • For the matchstick broccoli, cut the outside of broccoli stem off, then slice into thin boards and slice those again into matchsticks (as you would carrots).</li>
  • You can use an optional brussels sprouts if you can find them fresh, which they are not always available (don't use frozen, the texture will be soggy).</li>
  • You can make this salad and store it in separate containers in the fridge just like you get with a salad kit (See Image below: kale, cabbage and broccoli - bottom | cranberries and pepitas - middle | salad dressing - top).</li>
