Pizza is a meal that many families enjoy regularly, including my own.  It usually is one of those meals that I choose because I don't want to cook, I don't have a meal planned, my kids haven't been eating anything I make, I'm stressed, or my husband has a craving that can only be satisfied by pizza.  Really, I can come up with any reason to get pizza!

Even if you don't eat a lot of pizza, the same dough can be used for many different things.  You can make a filled dough ball or a pocket/calzone filled with anything from vegetables to sausage and eggs.

I have found that pizza dough is easier than most breads, especially when it comes to doughs with whole wheat.  The hardest part is the flour to water ratio which I find very temperamental depending on the season and humidity.  I suggest to start with the ratios provided, then add either a couple tablespoon of flour or a teaspoon of water at a time depending on if the dough is too wet or dry, respectively.  As time goes on you will be able to tell how much flour or water you need to add to get the right consistency.

KIM Tips</h2>

  • You can store the dough in the fridge for up to 3 days (it may take on some sourdough like flavor and smells, which is fantastic!)</li>
  • You can wrap extra dough in plastic wrap and put in a zip top bag and freeze it</li>
  • For an easy make ahead pizza, you can roll the dough into a pizza crust, bake at 450 for 10 minutes, let cool, wrap in plastic wrap and freeze.  When ready to cook, just top the crust with sauce, cheese and other toppings and bake at 450 until cheese is melted</li>
  • Whole wheat flour can go bad quickly.  Keep it fresh by buying bulk and only what you need for a couple weeks or store it in and air tight bag in the freezer.</li>
  • If your yeast does not foam in the first step, it has gone bad and the dough will not rise.  Replace your yeast and start again!</li>
