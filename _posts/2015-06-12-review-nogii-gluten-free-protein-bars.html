I recently received a box of gluten-free goodies!  This is a product line of protein bars, protein powder, and paleo bars called NoGii (pronounced noh-jee).  They are created by Elisabeth Hasselbeck (of The View fame) who struggled with nutrition and after years of working with physician and nutritionists she was diagnosed with Celiac disease.

The protein bars have no gluten, trans fats, hydrogenated oils, or high fructose corn syrup. What do they have? an ideal balance of protein, fat and carbs.  The paleo bars have no dairy, soy, HFCS, trans fat, gluten, or peanuts.  They are made from whole foods and have no added ingredients that you can't pronounce!  In addition, they have what are called Protein D'Lites which are an amazing little chocolate caramel bars that are loaded with protein, eat like dessert, and are a great portion size.

I am personally not gluten-free, however as Elisabeth Hasselbeck points out in her book even if you do not have celiac or a gluten intolerance there are other benefits for going gluten-free.  The one that sticks out to me is mental focus!  That is something that I regularly struggle with and am curious if being gluten-free would help me with that.  Although I have not chosen to be gluten-free, I have been adopting a healthier diet where I have decreased the amount of gluten I consume.

If I were gluten-free, these bars would definitely be my protein bar of choice!  They truly are delicious.  Everyone that I had try the bars liked them - including my mom who is mostly gluten-free, my sister who is very athletic, my kids, my husband, and some of his coworkers who are on strict diets.

</a>

I enjoyed the Chocolate Mint High Protein bar the best.  I noticed that after working out in the evening and eating one of these protein bar (versus a snack of yogurt or cheese) I had a lot more energy and mental focus for the next few hours.  So rather than coming home from a workout and plopping on the couch, I felt like cleaning the kitchen and doing work on my computer.

My one complaint is the amount of sugar that is in the bars.  Even though they choose better sources of sugar than white processed sugar or high fructose corn syrup, sugar is something that my family tries to keep to a minimum as we have seen how it affects our weight, energy, and food cravings.  For this reason, I personally would buy the paleo snack bars which are lower in sugar and from a whole food source.

In addition to the protein bars, I also got to try the quinoa protein powder!  It was fantastic.  Since it is summer I blended it with watermelon which was a wonderful combination.
