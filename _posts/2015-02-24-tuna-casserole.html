The reason I chose this recipe as one of the first to start my pantry clean out</a> is because it is probably the only reason I have cream of celery in my pantry!  This was a family favorite when I was growing up.  The only change that I had to make was using a regular pasta rather than the egg noodles my mom used.

In addition to using the cream of celery, I had actual celery that I needed to use.  Part of taking time out to clear out your pantry is not wasting any ingredients (or leftovers) before they go bad.  I still have some fresh vegetables that I purchased before deciding to go on a grocery shopping hiatus, and the celery and bell peppers were going soft.  Limp celery is not exactly appealing topped with peanut butter or dipped in hummus, but you never notice when its cooked.  When you buy ingredients like celery, plan ahead and know how you want to eat it raw and how you want to eat it cooked.  Start with the raw idea, then if you don't eat it all (or didn't have time for the raw recipe) go to the secondary cooked option.
