On any given Friday night, the smell of buffalo sauce wafts through our house.  There are so many recipes out there for buffalo wings.  Oven-baked, deep fried, pan fried, brined, marinated.  I tried so many different methods before I settled on one that I will stick forever because they are just as good as any restaurant wings.  In fact, they are more consistently delicious than the brewery that has some of the best wings in town!  For that reason it is worth the effort of these wings.  Really it is more time consuming than effortful.

First, the wings are brined in a simple solution of water, salt and baking soda for an hour.  Then they are sous vide for another hour.  Don't have a fancy machine?  Neither do I.  I set up a large pot of water on the stove, bring it to just above the cooking temperature, then add the wings.  Before I heat the water, I put the chicken in gallon zip top bags and dip them in the pot of water until the top is just above the water and close it.  This helps remove all of the air from the bag and essentially vacuum seals it without smooshing the chicken.  Why sous vide it? Same reason you sous vide anything.  It cooks the meat perfectly without it drying out or getting tough.

Once the chicken is cooked it is time to crisp up the outside.  I use a deep fryer, but you can also use a cast iron skillet with a half inch of oil and fry on each side for about 3-4 minutes.

Then the most satisfying part is tossing the wings in the sauce and watching them become a that familiar glorious orange color.
