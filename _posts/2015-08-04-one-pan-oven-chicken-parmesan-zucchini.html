You may be thinking "another breaded chicken recipe, does she cook anything else?".  Of course! I cook new things every week.  In fact, rarely do I repeat recipes unless I don't have time to menu plan</a>, I am doing a pantry clean out</a> (which I now do every other month), or my kids are sick.  However, I don't share recipes the first time I make it.  I typically want to make sure it is replicable and I want to put my own spin on it which takes time.  Not to mention most evenings I cook a meal I'd like to share with you, life happens and every bit of the dinner is eaten before I can take a picture!

It seems as if we are all dealing with a constant struggle with food.  Whether eating healthy, not overeating, or getting kids and husbands to try anything</em> new.  I have started researching the idea of food chaining to introduce new foods (for kids and adults!!).  It seems like a smart approach and doesn't involve begging, bribing, or starving our kids.

The idea of food chaining is, if your child will only eat chicken nuggets from McDonald, don't try to give them shredded chicken breast tossed in BBQ sauce and assume they will eat it.  Start with other chicken nuggets - other restaurants, frozen, then homemade</a>.  Then build on that and serve with sauces, like BBQ.  Build on it more and serve different flavors of breaded chicken (like my coconut breaded chicken</a>, tortilla crusted chicken</a>, or this chicken parmesan) and different sauces (like ranch, honey mustard, teriyaki</a>, or marinara). Then move on to plain cubed, then shredded, chicken or turkey, changing the seasoning and so on until you get to BBQ pulled chicken.  It can be a long process, but it beats some of the alternatives and provides better lasting results.

Although my kids are not that picky, it may be because I already was inherently using some of the food chaining principles in my day to day cooking for them.  This method is often used for kids with Sensory Processing Disorder, which my son does show some signs of and I love to help other mothers who struggle with their children's eating habits.  So, although simple, I want to provide tried and true recipes and directions for those mothers.

In addition, I also want to show you how to cook healthy meals in a way that is not intimidating. It is something that can be done by every home cook for every family.

I chose Chicken Parmesan because was one of the first dishes I learned how to make really well.  I started from the instructions on the tub of breadcrumbs and developed it into my own recipe.  I would say that it is a dish that helped me learn how to cook - how to deal with chicken breast, how to do a flour-egg-bread process, how to control the heat of a pan, and how to make a great sauce (marinara).

In the past I have typically served my chicken parmesan with a side of pasta.  However, that creates a meal that is heavily carb centered with makes me heavily centered around the belly.  So I decided I would sub the pasta for spaghetti squash and add zucchini.

Now that I’m a mom, meals also need to be simpler to prepare.   I love to sit at the stove and cook chicken parmesan, but that is a little more difficult now.  Not to mention adding two veggies to cook takes additional time.  So I decided to simplify and put it all in the oven!

Below is how to make a great, healthy dish simple. If you stay tuned all week I will let you in on what else I did tonight to make addition meals for the rest of the week without much additional work.

Here is your timetable:</h3>

  • Preheat oven to 400 - add spaghetti squash (split lengthwise in a baking dish with a little water) for 45 minutes.</li>
  • Prepare Chicken Parmesan (recipe below) and do a little clean up - with about 15 minutes remaining on the squash place the chicken in the oven on a baking sheet for 15 minutes.</li>
  • Prepare zucchini: cut into even rounds, then halve or quarter if large.  Toss with about 1/2 tablespoon of olive oil per each small zucchini, season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice.</li>
  • When the total 45 minutes is up, remove chicken & spaghetti squash from oven, flip chicken, spread prepared zucchini around the chicken (in the empty parts of baking sheet).  Bake for another 10 minutes.</li>
  • Check spaghetti squash for doneness (can be easily pierced with a fork or taste a small portion to see if the texture is how you like it) return to the oven if needed or let cool (do a little more clean up).</li>
  • Remove Chicken and Zucchini from the oven, and pull "noodles" from the spaghetti squash.</li>
  •  Plate each item, and serve dinner!  If you were efficient with your time you should have minimal clean up after you eat.</li>
