Burgers are not typically seen as "Seasonal" cooking.  Bread, meat, and condiments are available all year.  But why not add ingredients to your burger that are seasonal?

This burger does not appear to be different than your ordinary burger, but it has grated apple mixed through the patty.  Not pictured is caramelized onions which are a staple this time as it is a lengthy process perfect for cooking on long cold days.

If you fear dry turkey burgers, try to find a blend (or grind one yourself!) that uses both white and dark meat.  If you can't find a blend with dark meat don't worry, the addition of apples to the mixture not only adds flavor but also moisture.

Think you will miss your red meat? I use a steak or grill seasoning to add the savoriness you are looking for.

KIM Tips</h2>

  • If you want caramelized onions but don't want to hang in the kitchen for an hour while they cook, simply make them in your slow cooker or crock pot.</li>
  • If you are grating the apple by hand on a cheese grater, I would suggest peeling the apple first to make it easier to grate.  If you are using a food processor, leave the skin on (more fiber and flavor) but check for large pieces of skin.</li>
  • Depending on the size of your buns or how large you like your patties, you may want to make 4 for larger buns or thicker patties, or 6 for smaller buns or thin patties.</li>
  • Mix the ground meat as little as possible for the best texture.</li>
