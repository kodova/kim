If you are like me, the first time I heard of Texas Caviar I thought it was a dish with fish eggs in it.  That is because I am originally from Washington, down here in the southwest it is a well known dish.  It is called Texas Caviar simply because it originated in Texas and the black-eyed peas resemble caviar.

It may seem like a common man's dish, but the creator was a food consultant and cookbook author who directed the food service at Neiman Marcus.  It was first served on New Years eve, so it would be a great dish to take to a holiday party (and bonus, you can make it up to a week in advance!).

Texas Caviar is often served by itself or with tortilla chips.  I prefer to use a whole grain or blue tortilla chips for their flavor.  If you make a whole batch for yourself you might want to think of new ways to use it!  Top some salad greens for a simple salad (no dressing required if you have a little leftover dressing at the bottom of the bowl), toast some crostini and top, roll up in a soft tortilla shell.  How do you like your Texas Caviar?
