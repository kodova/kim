When I got all that cheap produce</a> this past weekend I sat down with my meal planner</a> to determine what veggies I wanted to create our dinners around this week.  The first thing that popped in my head was chile rellenos with the poblanos.  The second was chicken gyros with the tomatoes and cucumbers, which I was already thinking about making, I'll share that tomorrow.

I made a few changes to the traditional chile relleno - a roasted chile, stuffed with cheese, battered and fried.  First, I wanted this to be a main meal for my family without too many side dishes.  So I decided to stuff the chile with cheese, ground chicken (beef, pork, or turkey could be used as well) and diced tomatoes.

The assembly of a traditional chile relleno also needed to be changed.  The careful splitting and stuffing and closing with a toothpick was too tedious for the time I had and the likelihood that I would be interrupted by kids.  So instead I cut open the roasted pepper, removed the stem and seeds, stuffed it, and rolled/folded it back up.

To still get some batter, I took spoonfuls and spread it thinly on a cast iron skillet that had been heated with a little bit of oil - essentially making a batter round that I placed under the chile.  You need to keep it thin, or it will seem like a bad mix of fried and scrambled eggs!  As far as I am aware this isn't really a practiced method, but I had every intention of doing it the right way but realized I couldn't that particular evening after all the ingredients were prepared.

KIM Kid tip:  I served just the filling and the batter rounds to the kids.  They haven't particularly cared for spicy things lately so I omitted the pepper completely.  I will however introduce roasted poblanos to them in a less direct way.
