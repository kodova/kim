Chicken tenders are a staple for many families.  It seems like no matter if you have a picky eater or not, a favorite meal of kids is breaded chicken.  It is one of my go to meals.  When I find chicken tenders on sale, I stock up.  I make several batches of my regular Chicken Strips</a> and freeze them.  Whenever the mood strikes or we don't have a dinner plan, I pull them out of the freezer.

But if you give kids the same thing too frequently, they start to develop bad eating habits.  Some will only want to eat the same thing for every meal for day and days.

If you find your family in a situation where chicken strips</a> are regularly demanded and you are sick of it, then switch it up!  Try this coconut breaded chicken recipe.  It looks nearly the same as your traditional chicken strip recipe, but it adds a new flavor.  So if your kids are picky, this is a great way to introduce them subtly to the flavor of coconut.  It is sweet, so your kids are guaranteed to love it!  In addition, most kids are willing to try any dipping sauce - they are a familiar, smooth texture are frequently sweet.  This sauce is a great way to get familiar with mango, which is naturally sweet and a great source of nutrients.  Not to mention it is only three ingredients - mango, curry powder and a sweet chili sauce</a>.

Besides a three ingredient sauce, the recipe for the chicken is simple to make so it can be a quick weeknight meal.  The proportions for coconut, panko, and flour are the same - so no need to check the recipe over and over to see how much you need.  Bonus - you dirty fewer measuring cups!  To learn more about making chicken strips and how to freeze them, see my post on Homemade Chicken Strips</a>.

One quick note about why I prep my chicken the way I do - I have you pound out the chicken into a thin even strip rather than having big round pieces because this allows you to "shallow" fry rather than deep frying the chicken.  It is a bit simpler, less to clean up, and less oil wasted (unless you have a deep fryer).
