At times over the summer (and spring here in AZ) you can get corn for really cheap - I just got 5 ears for $1.  Although you can change up the seasoning you use for traditional corn on the cob, occasionally you need to switch it up.  So I grilled up some of the corn, made corn stock</a> with my husks and cobs, and put them together in a soup!

I've seen a lot of corn chowders with bacon, and as much as I love bacon, I felt like chorizo would add amazing spice without a lot of added ingredients or measuring.  The texture of chorizo I also felt would be better in soup - I don't like chewy, soggy bacon.

Depending on the chorizo you choose, this soup can be a little spicy.  The chorizo I chose ended up being a little too spicy for my little ones.  If it is a little spicy for you, a tablespoon of sour cream or a small handful of cheese helps.  And for adult - it pairs perfectly with a cold beer!

KIM Tip:  You can use fresh or frozen corn for this recipe.  If using frozen, take 1 1/2 cups thawed corn and "roast" them over a hot saute pan until you get a deep brown color on most of the kernels.
