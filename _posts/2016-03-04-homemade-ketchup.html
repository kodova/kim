Yesterday I wrote an Instruction Manual on Potatoes</a>. And what goes better with potatoes than Ketchup?  Homemade Ketchup!!!

As we all strive to eat healthier & cleaner, simple basics like ketchup may be overlooked.  Sometimes a good way to judge food is to ask would our (great)Grandparent eat that?  They grew up in an era where processed food was minimal.  But I bet our Grandparents ate ketchup - Heinz started producing ketchup in the late 1800s!  It has been around for a looooong time.   But what hasn't been around that long is High Fructose Corn Syrup, which is in almost every ketchup bottle on the shelf today.

Lets take ketchup back from the manufacturers and start making it ourselves.

Just like anything else you make from scratch, you can adjust the recipe and make it your own.  The one thing that will make the most impact on your ketchup is the amount of vinegar.  My family likes it on the tangy side, so we add a little more vinegar.  Other changes you could make: if you like it sweeter, add more sugar.  If you are eating clean/processed free/whole foods, you may swap the brown sugar for raw honey.  You can also play with the amount of spices and find the perfect fit for your taste buds!

KIM Tips

  • You could store the ketchup in a glass mason jar, or for easy access get a plastic squeeze bottle</a>!</li>
  • Cooking is optional!  You can make this ketchup in a blender. However, the flavor of the spices will come through more if you let it simmer on the stove.</li>
