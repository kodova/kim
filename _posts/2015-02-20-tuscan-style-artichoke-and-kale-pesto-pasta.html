It can be hard to get kids to eat dark green leafy vegetables.  Lettuces can be texturally difficult for kids to eat.  Green smoothies are a frequent favorites of moms, but kids (or whoever is the picky eater) need to see their greens in order to learn to like them.  Using a pesto or chopped parsley in pasta is a great way to start exposure.  Next step would be to add chopped greens (gradually getting larger) to the pasta, then remove the pasta all together and do cooked greens or a raw salad.

Pastas are also a great way to introduce new ingredients.  Whether to yourself, your picky spouse, or your kids.  Pasta with a familiar sauce and protein can mask a more unfamiliar ingredient.  Here I added artichoke.  I cut it up pretty small so its not as noticeable texturally or bold in flavor.  If you think the new ingredient might be rejected completely, then remove some of the dish before adding it or add very little that can be picked out.  The last thing you want is a "hangry" little one running around the house and for there to be more leftovers than you can eat by yourself.

 KIM Tips: </strong></h3>
