Monti Carlo, a local radio show host, put on this contest for the Arizona Health & Fitness Expo in Phoenix this past weekend.  My recipe (below) was chosen as one of the top 5 recipes submitted and I was asked to compete for the grand prize.  Monti is an inspiration for me - she is a mom and a home cook who made it to the top 5 of Gordon Ramsey's Master Chef competition.  Amazing!  Being a new mom is definitely an identity change, and cooking has linked my "old" self to my "mom" self.  Cooking is a stress reliever, my happy place, and has the end reward of amazing food!

The contest was to create a salad that is healthy and tasty.  Although I did not win, my salad was a crowd favorite.  There were gasps and yells of "What?!?" when the winner was announced and it was not mine.  Even Monti said she agreed with the outcry and that she thought I would have won.  Salmon is an excellent source of omega-3 fatty acids, and this salad provides one of the 2-3 recommended portions of fish a week.  But you could also use chicken or flank steak as the protein.  The dressing does not have any oil in it so the fat content is low.  The dressing also has a powerful flavor, so you do not need a lot of it.

For the competition I added a special molecular gastronomy touch to the salad.  I took a portion of the salad dressing and added agar-agar to it, dropped it into chilled vegetable oil with a pipette to make "caviar" dressing.  It is a salmon salad after all!!  The dressing balls allowed the flavor to pop in your mouth, so every few bites of salad you get a little more dressing.  I will post instructions with step by step pictures soon so you can try it - it was a simple way to impress!

As a new mom with a husband who is a picky eater, I rarely have time to cook anymore.  Most of what I cook from scratch these days is baby purees, but I still enjoy every moment in the kitchen (that isn't doing dishes).  I was hoping I could be a "naptime" chef and the big reason for the start of this blog.  However, that has yet to work out!!  This contest was inspiring, and I hope to post regularly in the future.
