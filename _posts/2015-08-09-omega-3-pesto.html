I made a pesto chicken salad</a> this week, and I realized I have only shared my Kale Pesto</a> recipe with.  It also reminded me of the short presentation I gave recently about depression and how the food we eat can affect how our body and brain work.  Some foods negatively effect our bodies while others, like the Omegas in this pesto, have a positive effect.  Here is a little of what I talked about in that presentation:

The parts of the brain responsible for regulating mood, thinking, sleep, appetite and behavior don't function normally in people who are depressed. In addition, important neurotransmitters-chemicals that brain cells use to communicate appear to be out of balance.

In order for those brain cells and neurotransmitters to function properly, your body needs phospholipids which are produced by Essential Fatty Acids - found in the  form of Omega-3 & Omega-6.  In addition, studies have show that increasing your intake of foods high in omega fatty acids decreases inflammation, which has been linked to depression.

Even if you aren’t depressed, Omega 3s have many other benefits such as being anti-inflammatory - which causes a number of problems and diseases -  improved heart health, and immunity.  Fish oil supplements can also help with post-workout soreness.

Nutritious food can help improve our mood and make us feel physically better.  When we feel healthy, are happy, and stress is minimal that is when we are in the best position to</b> flourish</b>…  So my goal for you is to start increasing your intake of omega-3s.

Pesto is a great way to eat sources of Omega-3s because its ingredient are good to excellent sources of Omega-3s: </strong></strong></em>Basil (good source of omega-3s</em>), Walnuts (excellent source of omega-3s</em>), Flax Oil (also an excellent source of omega-3s</em>, alternatively use ¼ cup more olive oil)

NOTE: If you use flax oil, do not heat pesto to a high temperature.  Rather toss cooked vegetables, meat, or pasta in pesto.
