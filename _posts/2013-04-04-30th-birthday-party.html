As a foodie and a home cook, I love to throw parties.  Or at least the idea of it - you get to show off your cooking skills, be with friends or family, and likely drink!  However, parties come with a lot of work if you are cooking a huge spread.  The last Super Bowl party we had, I spent days preparing the menu, shopping list, and cooking timeline.  Then spent a day grocery shopping (had to go to the asian market, the butcher, the regular grocery store, and Bed, Bath and Beyond) and two and a half days in the kitchen.  Exhausting! I barely wanted to walk around and talk to guests.

For my husband's 30th birthday, I decided to go a different route and barely cooked.  His birthday is in the middle of MLB Spring Training (and we live in Arizona!) so we decided to do a baseball themed party.  Sound childish?  Maybe a little, but my husband is almost childish about his love for baseball.

I bought a bunch of candy you would find at a concession stand and then spotlighted it with a couple of items that would make anyone forget that you only cooked two things!  We had hot dogs and nachos with all of the fixin's including a homemade "condiment" chili for the hot dogs, and pork carnitas for the nachos  (recipes to be posted at some later time).  Both of these recipes are so easy to make as they are slow cooked in a dutch oven, and can/should be made in advance to help all of the flavors build.

So next time you are looking to through a party but do not want to spend days prepping food, find two larger things that can be prepared in advance and then surround it with other store bought items.  Another example is a pizza party; prepare dough and pizza sauce days in advance and then provide half to a full dozen different toppings.
