This past summer my basil plant did not do as well as it normally does.  So this winter I did not have any pesto stored away in the freezer.  I came across the idea of using some winter greens in Bon Appetit</a>
magazine this month (February 2015).  They suggested using Arugula, Swiss Chard, or Kale.

A typical pesto uses basil, pine nuts, and pecorino or parmesan. Here I substituted kale, walnuts, and parmesan cheese respectively.

Just like regular pesto, this can be used in a variety of ways.  With pasta, on chicken, as a sandwich spread, even on pizza.  Here are some recipes to use this pesto:

Tuscan-Style Artichoke and Kale Pesto Pasta</a>
Tuscan Pizza</a>
Pesto Chicken Salad</a>
