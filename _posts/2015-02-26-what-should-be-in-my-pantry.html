Everyone has a different list of what should be in your pantry at any given time.  Sure, there are some ingredients that will be present on each list like olive oil and rice.  But if you purchased everything on every list you would have more than you truly need.

So how should you create your own Pantry Essential List?  Find out what your family eats on a day to day basis.  Create a list of your regular recipes and the ingredients in each of them.  How often you cook will determine how many recipes you should choose. Start with your top 5-10 recipes.

When buying ingredients, consider how long the ingredient will last.  Did you know that brown rice only lasts 6-8 months even unopened?  So if you only use 1 cup every other week don't buy multiple 5-pound bags when they are on sale.

What do I keep in my pantry?</h3>
Oils (olive & vegetable), Vinegars (white, red wine, and cider), Spices (anything & everything!)

Black and Garbanzo beans

Canned Whole Peeled Tomatoes

Rice, Quinoa, and Penne pasta

Whole grain bread, tortillas

Eggs

Vanilla Greek Yogurt, Shredded Cheddar Cheese, Parmesan Cheese

Ground Beef, Chicken Breasts, Chicken Stock, Tilapia, Bacon

Salad Greens, Bell Peppers, Cherry Tomatos, Onion, Garlic, Carrots

Frozen Corn, Peas

Lemons or fresh lemon juice frozen

 

Other Ideas:</h3>
Winter Squash (can last for months)
Tofu (lasts in the fridge for weeks)
Apples (again, can last a long time)
Frozen Fruits & Vegetables
Any meat that can be frozen
Canned Tuna
Nuts (peanuts, cashews, almonds, walnuts), Seeds (pepitas, sunflower), Dried Fruits
Asparagus (When stored in fridge in a cup of water, it can last two weeks!</em>)
