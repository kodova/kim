Grain bowls are "all the rage" right now.  They are a healthy and very adaptable way to eat healthy.  It is like an omelette, once you have the technique you can create whatever you pleases you most or with whatever you have in your fridge.

I will admit that I am proud I was able to create such a healthy meal while cleaning out my pantry</a>!  First I made this awesome Tahini Dressing</a> from the leftover bit at the bottom of the jar. Then, I had been storing dried chickpeas for a few weeks, just waiting for the "right" moment to try to cook them for the first time.  It was super simple (read: hands off!) and it was awesome to not feel grossed out by the slimy liquid in the cans.  The chickpeas also did not fall apart as I stir fried them like the canned versions often do for me.  But do not feel like you can't make this dish if you only have canned chickpeas.

Proud mommy moment: My son grabbed a chair and ate them directly off the pan while they cooled.  He would rather eat chickpeas than goldfish!  Oh, and he also insisted on taking his own pictures of our food :)  Foodie or Photographer?

KIM Tips:

  • This is a great way to use any leftover grains you might have: quinoa, brown rice, farro, barley, etc.</li>
  • If you want this to be just a grain bowl, add an extra 1 1/2 cups or so of cooked grains.</li>
  • Depending on how much dressing you like, you may want to toss the salad greens in additional dressing.</li>
  • Chickpeas and Garbanzo beans are the same thing!</li>
  • Tofu preparation: buy firm/extra firm tofu, slice into 8 slabs, press under cutting board topped with heavy items for 30 minutes.  Then dice into 1/2 inch cubes. If you pre sear the tofu or use oven baked tofu then add after the vegetables are soft.</li>
  • Tofu is a great pantry item.  It is cheap and stays good in the fridge for weeks.  So if you don't have a fresh protein or forgot to thaw one from the freezer, then you can easily use tofu!</li>
  • If you don't like tofu, you can easily substitute cooked chicken breast for the tofu.  Since making some recipes from the Salad Samurai I have learned to love tofu!</li>
