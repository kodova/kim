Whenever it is time to heat up the grills, Teriyaki seems to be a popular smell throughout the neighborhood.... maybe the frozen, pre-marinated bag of chicken from Costco or a store bought bottle of teriyaki sauce.  Even so many of my extended family BBQs have consisted of simple salads with grilled chicken marinated in bottled teriyaki sauce.  Its not terrible, in fact it is a quick and easy meal.  But I am a proponent of making as much as possible from scratch.  It is cheaper, healthier, and one less bottle to store in the "condiment" buckets in the fridge.  In the past I have discussed my determination of eliminating salad dressing (which I have not in over 6 months), but I also make many of my own condiments and all marinades.

Teriyaki is a simple mix of common ingredients, all of which you should keep in your healthy pantry</a> to make quick and flavorful meals:  soy sauce, orange juice (or mirin if you have it), honey, garlic and ginger (ginger freezes great by the way).

Ideas For Using Teriyaki Sauce

  • Marinade chicken, beef, or salmon then grill or bake.</li>
  • Grill pineapple then brush a thin layer of sauce on.</li>
  • Mix in brown rice for a quick side dish to another Asian entree.</li>
  • As a sauce for grilled or baked broccoli or carrots.</li>
  • Follow the proportions in my Instruction Manual for salad dressing and add apple cider vinegar and olive oil for any salad (choose vegetables like romaine, cabbage, green onions, carrots, and sesame seeds).</li>
  • Use in fish likes my Teriyaki Salmon Cakes</li>
    </ul>
     

    KIM Tips

    • Make a double batch and put a pound of chicken, beef, or salmon with 1/2-3/4 of a cup of sauce in a zip top bag and store in the freezer for a last minute dinner.</li>
    • For a thicker sauce to drizzle over top of your meal, put the ingredients in a small sauce pot and bring to a simmer.  In a small bowl mix 1 tablespoon of cornstarch or arrowroot powder with 1 tablespoon water and whisk until combined.  Add to the sauce pot and simmer until thickened.</li>
    • If you are using raw honey, melt so it can dissolve throughout the sauce.</li>
    • Strain out the ginger and garlic for a cleaner looking sauce.</li>
      </ul>
