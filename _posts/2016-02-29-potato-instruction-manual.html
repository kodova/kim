There is a lot to know about potatoes that you might not have considered.  See all of those cooking methods above?  You need to pick the right potato for each one!  A russet will disintegrate when braised.  A waxy potato will be  gummy boiled and used for mashed potatoes (no thank you!).  A starchy potato will create a different texture when baked (your standard baked potato) and a waxy potato will be equally as pleasant, but creamy.</div>

</div>

All potatoes fall into three basic categories: starchy, waxy, and all-purpose (and then there is sweet potatoes which we will discuss another time!).</div>

</div>

Buying, Storing, and Prepping</h2>

Many people thrive on potatoes when their grocery budget</a> is small as russets can be as cheap as 10 pounds for a dollar at certain times of year!</div>

</div>

Buy potatoes that are firm and unshriveled.  Preferably without soft spots or sprouts, but those can be cut away.  Store them in a cool dark place in a basket or loosely covered bag, up to a couple weeks (do not refrigerate!).  Potatoes should not be store with onions, as that can make both the potatoes and onions spoil quicker due to a chemical reaction.  Also, check your potatoes occasionally and remove any that are starting to go bad as one rotten potato will also make the rest go bad.</div>
 

If you need to prepare potatoes in advance, you can cut them and place in a bowl covered in water for up to 24 hours.  Cutting potatoes in advance will cause them to be discolored, even after cooking.</div>
 

Baked potatoes are a common and easy way to prepare potatoes. It can also be healthy.  It is even easier if you are baking your dinner and you can place them on the side.  Here is a quick guide on how long to bake your potatoes at a given temperature:</div>
 

45 minutes at 400 degrees</div>

60 minutes at 350 degrees</div>

90 minutes at 325 degrees</div>

120 minutes at 275 degrees</div>

</div>
 

KIM Tips:</div>

  • When checking to see if a potato is done cooking, use a fork not a knife.  Any well sharpened knife will go into a potato easily, so a fork is a better way to test.</li>
  • If your potatoes are small enough, boil them whole and unpeeled.  This way they will be lighter in texture and will not absorb as much water.</li>
