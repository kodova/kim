</a>I was standing at the meat and seafood counter at my local grocery store looking for a protein to cook for dinner.  The butcher came out and asked if he could help me pick something out.  He mentioned that the salmon had come in 30 minutes ago and smelt fresh of watermelon.  SOLD.  After paging through some cookbooks and surfing around online, I decided to mash up a few recipes in order to use some spinach and blueberries that were on sale.

Don't be intimidated by the long ingredient lists.  The spice rub for the salmon has a complex taste, both bright and earthy, but only require one step of mixing and smashing (which is always a good way to get rid of stress!).  The dressing you basically through in a blender and let it go.  And the salad is simple tossed once you have the veggies cut.

KIM Tip: if you do not have a mortar and pestle to grind the spices with, you can either use a clean coffee grinder or place in a zip top bag, set on a kitchen towel (to dampen noise and keep the bag in place), and pound with a heavy object - rolling pin, meat pounder, or pot.
