A standard healthy snack is apples and a couple tablespoons of peanut butter.  Delicious.  The problem?  That it is recommended to eat a couple tablespoons</em>.  I want to eat a tablespoon on each slice!

The solution?  Mix in greek yogurt!! It still tastes like peanut butter, it has a creamier texture, and less goes further.  You get also get the added health benefits of the yogurt and less fat and calories than if you were having just PB.

Great idea, but you are too busy and don't have time to make a dip - right?  Think again.  This dip is only two ingredients!  And you can buy the individual size portions of greek yogurt so you have one less item to measure and one less measuring cup to clean.

Use whatever kind of peanut butter is your favorite - chunky or smooth.  You can even add crushed nuts to the top.  Just be sure to choose a healthy peanut butter.  Watch out for added sugars and hydrogenated oils.  Ideally "Roasted Peanuts" should be the only thing on the ingredient list on the nutrition label.

Same thing with yogurt.  Look for options that are only pasteurized milk and live active yogurt cultures.  Fat is OK!  Reduced fat options often have added sugars to make up for flavor.

For and idea of what to look for on your nutrition lables, my friends (and personal healthy eating inspiration) at Little Choices Matter</a> have brief posts on the nutrition labels of Greek Yogurt</a> and Peanut Butter</a>.
