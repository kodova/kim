If you joined me at Faith and Food in September, you will remember I did a cooking demo of a butternut squash recipe and provided 5 different ways to use the squash.  This was one of those recipes, and I'm sharing for all of those who missed the class!

So often when you think about adding vegetables to smoothies, green is the first thing that comes to mind.  Sometimes I just want a bright colorful smoothie that has no hint of green color or flavor!  This smoothie recipe uses squash as the vegetable rather than spinach.  It also has perfect fall flavor, reminding you of pumpkin pie.

KIM Tips</h3>
Use your leftovers!</strong>  If you made a squash soup</a> or even baby food, use any leftover squash puree for the smoothie.

Cook with your kids! </strong>This recipe is easy enough for your kids to dump all the ingredients in the blender or just push the button.  They will be more likely to try and love it!

Use your slow cooker </strong>to easily prepare your squash: wrap it in foil, place in empty slow cooker, and cook for about 4 hours (depending on the size of your squash and slow cooker).
