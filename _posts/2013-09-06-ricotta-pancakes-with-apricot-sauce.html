Often pancakes require a glass of OJ on the side because even drenched in syrup they leave the mouth feeling a little dry.  These pancakes have ricotta cheese to make them moist but not gooey or cheesy.  I am not one that enjoys a bowl of ricotta with pepper or fruit, the texture bothers me.  But if you didn't know what was in them, you probably wouldn't notice the ricotta.  Ricotta also adds nutritious benefits to pancakes; it has a large amount of protein per serving as well as being a good source of vitamins and minerals including calcium.

These pancakes are delicious with just a little butter or maple syrup.  But why not mix it up a little and top it with a fruit sauce?  It is tasty and a great way to get another serving of fruit in your or your family's diet.  You can use either fresh, frozen, or canned fruit for this topping.  I chose to use organic apricots packed in 100% fruit juice because that is what I had in my pantry.  If using fresh fruit, puree a couple apricots with some water to create a smooth texture.
