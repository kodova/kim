Part of the way I cook, is to focus primarily on using the ingredients I already have on hand because I believe it is the responsible way to use the food that is available to us. Even for this blog - I usually don't go out of my way to make a particular recipe because I think you want to see it.  I sit down every week, review what is in my pantry, fridge, and freezer, I menu plan</a> for my family, and then choose the best of those recipes to share with you.

Today's recipe for Roasted Red Pepper Hummus came from a need to use all the red peppers I roasted and froze</a> a few weeks ago as well as needing to feed my growing toddler who seems to be eating constantly.

I know I recently shared a recipe for edamame hummus</a>, but hummus is a healthy snack that easy to make and encourages you to eat a snack of vegetables.  So it is good to know several different ways to make hummus.  Summer is coming, and we all want to eat a little healthier and be more fit for those tank tops and shorts.  Many of you will have more mouths to feed while kids are on break from school.  However, we all get bored of that hummus with crudités like I have pictured above.  For that reason, I made this hummus and hope to find new and creative uses for it to make you excited about hummus again!

</h2>

New Ideas to use Hummus:</h2>
Spread in celery like you would peanut butter

Cut cucumbers crosswise in 1/2 inch chunks, scoop out some of the center, and place a small spoonful of hummus in the cup

Spread on deli meat (such as turkey), place slices of avocado on one end, roll and slice.  Optional: pierce with pretzel sticks to keep together and as a good snack for kids.

Cut tops off of mini bell pepper and stuff with hummus. Optional: garnish with cilantro.

Cut grape tomatoes in half and fill with hummus. Optional: garnish with basil, parsley, or cilantro.

Toss with steamed broccoli in place of cheese sauce.

Make a sandwich with hummus, sliced chicken or turkey, tomatoes, smashed avocado, baby spinach, and feta.

Spread on chicken breasts and bake at 400 for 30-40 minutes (or fish for 10-12 minutes) in a greased baking dish covered with foil.

Make an instant salad dressing by blending hummus, vinegar, and salt and pepper.

    KIM Tip - Homemade Roasted Peppers</h3>
    Since I have so many roasted peppers in my freezer, I don't have a photo step-by-step on how to roast peppers but here is a quick instruction:

    • Place peppers over an open flame  (outdoor grill, gas stove, broiler, or even blow torch) until charred</li>
    • Move the hot pepper to a bowl and cover with foil or plastic wrap for about 10 minutes or until cool enough to handle.  Alternatively place in a brown bag.</li>
    • Once cooled, remove the charred skin of pepper by peeling with your fingers or with a spoon.</li>
    • Pull off the stem and remove any seeds.</li>
    • Use in a recipe.  To store, place one cup portions in a freezer zip top bag. Spread evenly and freeze flat for easy and organized storage.</li>
