I have this problem - I know green tea is extremely good for me, but I can't drink it.  I love my morning coffee and I'm not willing to give that up (I've tried!) and</em> I live in Arizona.  If I wanted to drink tea in the afternoon it is way too hot most of the year (its September and still over 100 degrees).

In case you aren't aware, here are some of the health benefits of green tea:

  • Can help with weight loss by increasing metabolism</li>
  • Helps regulate blood glucose levels</li>
  • May help prevent heart disease and some forms of cancer</li>
  • Reduces bad cholesterol while raising good cholesterol</li>
  • Prevent mental decay in diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's</li>
  • Has strong anti-viral and anti-bacterial agents</li>
  • Can help with memory by improving the brain connections that process and store information</li>
    </ul>
    And again I wonder why it took me so long to start drinking green tea every day!

    I ran across this article from Prevention</a> that discusses the health benefits of hot, cold, and bottled teas.  They found that a cold brew method for tea (similar to cold brew coffee</a>) actually produces slightly more antioxidants than hot brewed tea.  So, as simple as it may sound, I realized that I could drink iced tea and still get all the benefits!

    If you are currently using a hot or sun brewed iced tea consider trying this cold brew method.  You don't have to heat up any water so it takes less time and energy and it does not have the risk of bad bacteria you may get from the sun brewed method.

    </a>

    You could make a simple green tea, but lemon and mint add additional health benefits not to mention make the tea taste great without any added sweeteners.  Lemon has vitamin C which helps the absorption of the health packed tea catechins.  In addition lemon is a natural antiseptic, helps the liver detox, and aids in digestions.  Mint also aids digestion, and may help with seasonal allergies and help break up congestion due to a cold.

    If you don't care about all of the health benefits of this tea, you should still make it so you can add a splash of rum to make a green tea mojito!!
