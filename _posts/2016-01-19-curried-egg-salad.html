I received several loafs of bread for free one day and realized I was going to need to come up with some new sandwiches.  I have nothing against the traditional egg salad, but it wasn't something that I was craving or felt particularly healthy about.  I came across the idea of a curried egg salad and what was appealing to me, besides the curry powder and cumin, because of the addition of granny smith apples.  The apples add a nice crispness to the creaminess of the eggs and greek yogurt (which I substituted for the traditional mayonnaise).

This is a great protein packed lunch. You can wrap it in a tortilla and pack for work or school.  Or even wrapped in lettuce or straight from a bowl for a carb/gluten free option!

If you get your kids involved in making this, or any, recipe they will be more likely to eat it.  I do most of the cutting (onions and apple) and let my son (3 years) use an egg slicer</a> to "dice" the eggs and then he helps measure and mix everything together.

It stays fresh for several days which is great for weeks that you know you need to meal plan</a> and prep over the weekends.

There are plenty of tricks to create the perfect hard boiled egg.  I simply use this Norpro Egg timer</a>.  You fill you pan up with water, eggs, and this timer.  As you cook your eggs the timer gets darker and once it gets to the line you desire (soft/medium/hard) you pull out your eggs and toss them in a bowl of ice water.  This allows me to walk around and do other things and never have to be precise about when to turn off the heat or drop the eggs into boiling water!  The only time I have overcooked my eggs and got the unpleasant greenish color around the outside was when I forgot about the eggs!
