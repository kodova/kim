Clean eating is more than just a trend, it is a lifestyle change that we all need to make.  But it needs to be one step at a time so you don't get overwhelmed by changes to the food you eat or cook.  One change that you can make that is easier than it may sound is to make your own sausage!

Sausage is a processed meat, nitrates and nitrites are added as a preservative.  Once in the body, they can be converted into cancer-causing compounds.  In addition, most sausage is made from pork, which more and more health experts are talking about how it is a toxic meat.  Then, there is salt.  Sausage contains a lot of salt - it is kind of what makes it taste so good!  But I think we all know that, especially as Americans, we consume too much salt.

A simple way to solve all those issues is by making your own sausage.  You can control the amount of salt added (so if it is a problem for you, you can add less).  You can be sure your sausage does not have added chemicals.  You can use turkey or chicken rather than pork.  And you can adjust the seasoning to be perfect for your tastebuds.

So, is making sausage difficult? No!  Take a pack of ground turkey, about 4 spices, and mix together.  Thats it.

You can also use ground chicken or even pork if it is organic/grass fed/no hormones added, etc.  I try to grind my own meat at much as possible, it is often cheaper and you can make sure there is nothing added (pink slime!) to the ground meat.  Simply cut any meat into chunks, freeze meat and grinder parts for about 15 minutes, then put the meat through the grinder according to it's instructions.  I use an attachment for my KitchenAid stand mixer</a>. I love to use ground chicken in my Buffalo Chicken Burger</a>.

KIM Tips

  • I use 20 ounces of ground turkey in this recipe as that seems to be a common amount sold.  You can easily adjust the amount of seasoning if you are only making a pound (if you don't want to do the math, just make all of the seasoning and only use about 3/4 of the seasoning).</li>
  • Make breakfast sandwiches with this sausage!  Portion about 2.5-3 ounces per patty (about 7-8 patties).  You can freeze any extras - just simply stack with a piece of parchment between each patty.</li>
