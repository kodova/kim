As I try to find quick and healthy meals for my family, it sometimes requires using leftovers from a previous meal.  Unlike most kids - my son does not like to eat the same thing within the typical timeframe leftovers are good for (even if he loves the dish).  So I am either required to eat leftovers by myself while I cook something new for my family or to reinvent the leftovers.

In a previous post, I shared how to make a Teriyaki sauce</a> or marinade.  I used that for a large portion of salmon, way more than my family would eat at one meal.  But I was thinking ahead and thought that I would use the salmon in salads (like my Thai Salmon Salad</a>).  Then I got an idea to make a salmon cake out of my leftovers, which sounded better than having a salad!

I used literally all of the leftovers for the previous meal - salmon, rice, broccoli, and teriyaki sauce.  And what was great about making a fish cake? My son who barely ate any of the dinner the night before ate the whole cake and liked it!  But don't worry, you don't need to have leftovers to make this dish- see the tips below.

 

KIM Tips:

  • This recipe could be adjusted for almost any cooked fish, rice, and vegetable combination you have - just keep the proportions the same.</li>
  • You can easily use two 6-oz cans or one 13 oz pouch of salmon.</li>
  • If you have time,  put the patties in the fridge for about 15 minutes to help the patties stay together.</li>
  • Really work/massage the mixture together to make a patty that will stick together.  If you don't like to touch the mixture you can try to pulse it in a food processor.</li>
  • After breading the patties, if they start to fall apart continue to work the patty into a disc and then re-bread it (breadcrumbs act as a binding agent, helping the mixture stay together but can make a fish cake dense if there is too much throughout).</li>
  • You could always put this patty on a burger bun! Or between lettuce leaves if you like that ;)</li>
  • Start with one tablespoon of the sauce.  If your mixture seems dry and crumbly, add up to one more tablespoon (one teaspoon at a time, 3 tsp = 1 tbsp).</li>
  • Flip the patties only once!  The more your turn them, the more likely it is they will fall apart.</li>
  • If you want to serve this as an appetizer, make 12 patties instead of 6.</li>
