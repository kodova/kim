This is a Take-Out, Fake Out that goes beyond your traditional fake-out because this dish is also vegetarian!  Only recently have I made an effort to eat meals without meat and at times it is a struggle to feel like I am eating a complete meal.  I don't like meat substitutes - not necessarily the flavor or texture, but the concept.  If I want to eat turkey, I will eat turkey and not tofurkey.  Whatever I am eating, I want the food to show itself and not pretend to be something else.

The chickpeas are tender, savory, and a little nutty - not at all like chicken, but you still don't miss it.  This is a great meal to try if you are not used to eating vegetarian dishes because it is a well balanced meal (Whole grains, vegetables, and protein/legumes) which will leave you satisfied.  You could also easily substitute in your favorite meat (See tips below for how to substitute meat for part or all of the chickpeas.

 KIM Tips</h2>
Make-Ahead Tip: Make the sauce up to several days in advance, store in an airtight container in the fridge. Cut vegetables and store in a container with a little water to prevent vegetables like carrots and celery from drying out.

Do not stir-fry the chickpeas for too long!  They will become gritty and grainy on the inside.  So stir-fry until they just start to get some color (doesn't need to be on all sides of the chickpea).

Substitution: You can easily replace all or some of the chickpeas with cubes of cooked chicken or thin slices of cooked beef.  If you are serving both a vegetarian and a carnivore, remove half the vegetables and add in a portion of chickpeas and sauce, toss to combine, then remove to serve.  Return the other half of the vegetables to the pan, add the meat and toss with remaining sauce.

Make it more restaurant quality: strain the sauce, so it is clean of the ginger and garlic pieces.  This will improve the look and texture.  Use a Fine Mesh Strainer</a> for best results.

Use it up challenge:  Use the garlic and ginger that you strained from the sauce to create a salad dressing.  Mix in oil and vinegar and shake.  See my Instruction Manual: Salad Dressing</a> for an idea on how much and what oil and vinegars to use (as well as many more ideas to help you create salad dressings)
