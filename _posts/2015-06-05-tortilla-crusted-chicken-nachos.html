Tortilla crusted chicken is an idea that has been around for a while and is on a lot of blogs.  But I still want to share my version with you because its awesome *wink*.

Tortilla crusted chicken is simple, cheap, and kids love it.  However, I find it a little one-note and salty.  When a meal disappoints me, I always think about what I can change to bring it to the next level.  I had a couple ideas involving salsa or enchilada sauce, then I realized my favorite thing to do with tortilla chips is.... Nachos! So why not smother it with cheese, black beans, (homemade) pickled jalapeños</a>, salsa, avocado and whatever your favorite nacho toppings are.

To make this a dish that your kids love even more- make it a build your own! After baking the chicken, let each family member top their chicken with whatever variety they like.  If you aren't serving a side of vegetables be sure to have diced tomatoes, onions, or whatever vegetable they might be willing to scoop on.

Not only is this a fun dinner, I think tortilla crusted chicken is an important recipe for everyone to know.  Why is it important</em>? Well, first because any recipe that your kids will eat is important!  Second, tortilla chips are something that many of us regularly eat so we often have them in our pantry, so this a perfect go-to meal when you don't have anything planned.  Third, most of us probably do not polish off the bag, and those broken bits at the bottom often get tossed in the trash with the plastic.  If you know me or have been following me (pinterest</a>, instagram</a>, and twitter</a>), I have been making a significant effort to not waste food and to cook "root to stalk" since I did a pantry clean out</a> and assessment of my own food waste.  This is a great recipe to make sure I'm not wasting even little scraps or crumbs of food.  This method uses those chip crumbs that aren't capable of dipping! It also works with stale chips (baking refreshes them a little), so if you find a bag of stale chips in the back of your cabinet you can use those too.

 

KIM Tips

You can use whatever chicken you prefer or have available (you can mix them up too!):

  • Chicken tenders: Cover with plastic wrap or put in a zip top bag, use a mallet or heavy pan to pound out evenly.</li>
  • Chicken breast: If really thick, butterfly or slice the breast in half so you have two thin pieces. Cut again into smaller strips about 1 1/2 inches wide.  As with the tenders, pound out (if necessary) to reach about 1/2 inch even thickness.</li>
  • Chicken thighs: Remove skin and bone and lay flat, again pounding out if you have any uneven spots.  Cut in half to create a more "chicken strip" shape.</li>
    </ul>
    Although it might be a habit to salt your chicken, I would skip this step or salt lightly because tortilla chips are very salty.

    The method to prepare the chicken is just like a typical breaded chicken for chicken strips or chicken parmesan.  If you are unfamiliar with the process, read more about it on my Chicken Tenders</a> post.

    If you don't have enough chip crumbs either freeze them in a zip-top bag adding to it whenever you have crumbs or try a substitute like another chip variety or even breadcrumbs/panko.

    If you are using a shredded cheese rather than a nacho cheese sauce, sprinkle on the cheese then melt under the broiler as the microwave may make the chip crust soggy.

    To reheat any leftovers place on a sheet pan and broil until heated through and crispy.
