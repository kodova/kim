In the past I have attempted to make iced coffee only to be disappointed.  Since then I have just avoided attempting it at home, might as well spend the money and get something that tastes good and isn't gritty.  Not anymore!</p>

Coffee is normally brewed at hot temperatures that extract the best flavors and aromas, but it also brings out bitterness.  There is a part of our tastebuds that are sensitive to temperature and affect how we perceive flavors.  So when coffee is brewed hot it has a bitterness that is appreciated when consumed hot but not warm or cold.  At lower temperatures that bitterness stands out and hides the amazing flavors of coffee.</p>

This problem is solved by a method called cold brewing.  You get the flavor of hot coffee without the bitterness.  Because the water is cold or room temperature it takes longer to extract the flavor from the coffee beans than hot water.  So it is best to let the mixture of water and coffee grounds brew overnight.  But if you make a large portion it keeps in the fridge so you can brew once and enjoy multiple times!</p>

I just started using an Eva Solo</a> for all of my coffee brewing.  It brings out flavor in coffee like I never imagined could be possible at home.  You could also use a french press, but all you really need is a pitcher and a cheesecloth lined strainer.</p>
