For my first year of gardening I have picked a couple of simple things to grow: basil, mint, and (easy to grow in Arizona) Jalapeños.  The jalapeño plant has left me with more jalapeños than I can use fresh.  I love pickled jalapeños but have had a hard time finding a store brand that I enjoy, so I am excited to try it myself.  Plus, I am trying to have a more homemade pantry and this is a simple recipe to replace a store-bought staple.

If you are new to canning, this is a good place to start as you do not need to submerge the filled jars in water so you need less equipment and time.  You simply sterilize the jar in boiling water fill with the peppers and brine and then cap and wait for the lid to "pop".  You can check to see if this was successful by pressing the center of the lid; if it presses down and releases back up it is not sealed properly.  However, if it does not give then it is sealed.  As I am not an expert on canning, please use your senses when you open the jalapeños.  If they do not look or smell right, then don't eat them!

This is a small batch to fill one pint jar or two half pints.  You can easily double the recipe if your family uses a lot of jalapeños.
