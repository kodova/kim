I have challenged myself to clean out my pantry</a>.  Beyond just using the ingredients I have, I am trying not to waste anything.  It is even a trend in the food world - nose to tail (for cooking with meat) and root to stalk (for cooking with vegetables).  It may not be as cool to talk about the random mess of the jars in your pantry, but it is still important to help minimize food waste.

Today I will show you how to take the last few tablespoons of Tahini that stick to the jar and make a great salad dressing</a>!  It is so good, you wont want to wait until your tahini is almost gone!

This recipe only has a few ingredients that you can measure directly into the tahini jar!  The most important part to make this dressing successful is to pour in hot water, put on the lid and shake.  You may need to open the jar to scrape the bottom or whisk if you had a really big chunk.

Use this dressing on a salad or with whole grains (such as brown rice, barley, or quinoa) or a combination of both in my Tofu Chickpea Grains & Greens bowl.

For more information and instructions on salad dressings see my post "Instruction Manual: Salad Dressing"</a>.
