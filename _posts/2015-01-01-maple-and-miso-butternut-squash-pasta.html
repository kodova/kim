</a>I have seen an overwhelming amount of recipes using miso</a> lately.  I previously had looked for miso at the grocery store and couldn't find it, but because of its growing popularity I recently stumbled upon it and couldn't resist the purchase.  I went home and immediately looked for a recipe to cook.  I found a few recipes that looked appealing and combined a few ideas.  What I came up with is a recipe that has very few ingredients, use pantry staples, and is delicious (once you have miso it can last for up to a year in the fridge).

Walnuts and butternut squash pair nicely together in this recipe, however they can easily be substituted.  Any winter squash (like acorn or pumpkin) could be a stand-in, while almonds or hazelnuts can replace the walnuts.

This pasta could easily be a side or a meatless main dish.  It might seem like a lot of work to cook a butternut squash for a side dish especially since you only need a small portion of the puree.  However, cooked squash can stay in the fridge for about 5 days or in the freezer for 6 months and you can use the leftovers for another recipe such as my Acorn Squash Soup</a> or even for baby food.  And remember, you can store a winter squash for a couple of months!  So always keep one on hand.

KIM Tips</h2>

Miso</h3>
Check out my Instruction Manual: Miso</a> post if you are not familiar with the ingredient. If you haven't tried miso or are intimidated by the idea of a fermented soybean paste, then this is a good recipe to start with as miso adds a subtle flavor and is easy to work with.  If you don't have miso on hand, I'm sure you can omit it. Just taste and add more syrup for sweetness or more salt if the flavors seem dull.

Walnuts</h3>
Why toasted walnuts?  Walnuts are naturally a little bitter when they are raw and toasting them decreases the bitterness and helps bring out the earthiness.  You can either put the walnuts on a rimmed baking sheet in a 375 degree oven for 5-10 minutes, or on the stove in a dry, hot saute pan and stir frequently until fragrant for 1 to 2 minutes.
